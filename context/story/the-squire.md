# The Squire (Starting Archetype)

- Status: developing player-character context
- Decision: [DEC-0009](../decisions/index.md#dec-0009-starting-archetype-character-creation)

## Role

**Squire** is a starting archetype (base class), not the fixed identity of the protagonist. Players who choose Squire begin as a melee trainee on the Cristallo-aligned noble-house path.

See [Character Creation](character-creation.md) for the shared protagonist premise and the other starting archetypes.

## Background

A Squire begins as an ordinary person from a settlement under the Kingdom of Tessera. King Asher the Brittle has ordered every able-bodied person into the war against the Chaotic Imperium. The character is drafted before escaping and sent to the front lines after the Imperium breaches the supposedly impenetrable fortress of Calrenoth.

Asher replaced the remembered “Great King” Luceran, whose fall and transformation into the Usurper are central to the conflict.

**Twine Act 0 draft:** Creotar’s vision calls Asher Luceran’s **fragile half-brother** on the throne ([`sources/wrathful-conquest-act0.twee`](sources/wrathful-conquest-act0.twee)). Grenge also complains that since Asher assumed the throne he stalls and withholds support (the player may arrive alone instead of a promised battalion). Treat half-brother kinship and “brittle / absent support” as **draft** until owner locks Luceran–Asher kinship.

## Goal and Social Obligation

The Squire is expected to satisfy a noble house or tribe, either by gaining power for that group or improving its influence. The character's path develops through allegiance to the Cristallo-aligned noble houses or to the tribes.

The archetype begins from fear and obligation rather than conventional heroic confidence. With no easy way out of the war, the player decides what to make of the predicament.

## Progression

- Squire to Knight or Warrior/Barbarian, depending on faction reputation.
- Later archetypes emerge from subclass, morality, and allegiance milestones.

Detailed advanced archetypes are deferred until after the demo under the current plan.

## Leadership Struggle

A Squire player may grow into a future leader or king on either a good or evil path. That rise forces decisions about preserving, reforming, or dismantling the Cristallo and tribal factions. Relationships and faction standing determine which options remain viable and what consequences follow. Charisma strongly influences how others judge these decisions.

## Relationships

Companion selection directly affects the protagonist's relationships and development. The story is intended for an M-rated audience and may include mature relationships and emotionally intimate or quasi-romantic bonds depending on character development.

## Open Questions and Conflicts

- A second opening concept says a Wild God revives the protagonist after their settlement burns. This may follow the draft and fortress deployment, but the chronology is not yet established.
- Define King Asher's legitimacy, personality, and relationship to Luceran (Twine half-brother draft is not established).
- Define noble houses, tribes, and the precise obligation created during character creation for Squire players.
