# Story Vision and Campaign Structure

- Status: developing story context
- Working campaign title: *Wrathful Conquest*

## Genre and Tone

The campaign is medieval dark fantasy with a medium- to low-fantasy baseline. Its dramatic core is serious, but the presentation uses comedy and deliberately goofy personalities as contrast. The intended outer shell can feel humorous while invested players discover a darker story underneath.

The art style and comedy should reinforce one another. Dark player choices may produce dark comedy, while lighter paths can support more playful humor. Characters may have distinct comedic styles rather than sharing one uniform voice.

## Central Player Fantasy

Tessera exists independently of the player; the protagonist is native to the setting rather than transported into it. A major kingdom has fallen from grace under an overt supernatural evil. The player can oppose that evil, exploit it, or ultimately become a greater evil.

The player's choices influence two initially neutral factions, potentially turning them toward good or evil. Relationships and companions give those choices personal consequences in addition to political ones.

Character creation at new-game start is defined in [DEC-0009](../decisions/index.md#dec-0009-starting-archetype-character-creation). The player customizes a Tessera-native protagonist and selects a starting archetype—**Squire**, **Archer**, or **Acolyte**—rather than always playing a Squire.

## Morality and Allegiance

The campaign uses a definitive good-versus-evil axis:

- Choices move the player along a morality track.
- Reaching thresholds on that track unlocks class archetypes.
- Archetype selection binds the player to a neutral faction under the current proposal.
- Charisma influences how other characters receive difficult political and moral decisions.
- The ending and portions of the storyline change with the player's moral alignment.

## Draft Campaign Flow

Superseded for structure by the [Campaign Beat Sheet](campaign-beat-sheet.md) ([DEC-0021](../decisions/index.md#dec-0021-soft-gates-with-rare-optional-instances)): acts are narrative arcs on the seamless world with soft gates and rare optional instances — not chapter load screens.

Historical imported outline (kept for provenance):

1. Start a new game and watch the opening cutscene.
2. Customize the protagonist, select a starting archetype (Squire, Archer, or Acolyte), and select difficulty.
3. Enter a tutorial battlefield through a second cutscene.
4. Meet major antagonists and an initial companion.
5. Learn basic combat, turret, and battlefield mechanics.
6. Use a basic dialogue tree to meet an NPC guide.
7. Retreat to a camp after the Great Evil drives the party from the tutorial area.
8. Reach the first village or town and unlock the wider campaign structure.

Act 0 working spine (Twine-backed, **draft**): Calrenoth siege with Arkand → Commander Grenge → drawbridge → Luceran collapse → Creotar vision. See [campaign-beat-sheet.md](campaign-beat-sheet.md).

## World and Locations

**Tessera** is the world’s primary land — the Middle-earth-scale geography of the campaign ([DEC-0034](../decisions/index.md#dec-0034-tessera-is-the-worlds-primary-land)). The **Kingdom of Tessera** is one polity within that land, not the land itself.

Tessera includes high mountains, prairie, farmland, woods, and chaotic regions warped by supernatural forces. Candidate location types include towns, castles, cities, villages, groves, markets, islands, docks, plazas, bazaars, battlefields, forests, hills, and mountains.

## Difficulty-Dependent Story

The source material proposes “softcore” and “hardcore” modes that can alter story outcomes. In particular, companions may die permanently in hardcore mode.
